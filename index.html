SIM-School Innovation Marathon

School Innovation Marathon is envisioned as India’s largest school innovation challenge organized jointly by the Ministry of Education, Atal Innovation Mission (AIM) NITI Aayog and Ministry of Education’s Innovation Cell (MIC), where students from all schools of the country identify community problems of their choice and develop innovative solutions in the form of working prototypes. The Top Teams of School Innovation Marathon will get funding support from the Ministry of Education.

Last Year Highlights :
  • 19000+ Innovations witnessed in last edition of Marathon.
  • Internship opportunities for Top 400 teams through SIP program.
  • Mentorship Program for Top 75 teams through SEP program
  • Prizes and certificates from AIM, NITI Aayog to students.

Viksit Bharat 2047 - Themes for SIM 2024-25

The vision of "Viksit Bharat 2047" aims to transform India into a developed nation by 2047. To support this, the School Innovation Marathon 2024-25 has set broad themes. Start your research to generate innovative ideas and implement solutions.

Sustainable Development


This theme emphasizes the importance of balancing economic growth with environmental protection to ensure a sustainable future for coming generations.

Focus Areas


  • Environmental conservation
  • Renewable energy
  • Sustainable agriculture
  • Water management

Digital Transformation


Highlights the critical role of digital technologies in bridging the digital divide and fostering innovation, making India a leader in the digital economy.

Focus Areas


  • Digital literacy
  • Access to technology
  • Cybersecurity
  • AI technology based ideas

Health and Well-being


Focuses on enhancing the overall health and wellness of communities, ensuring that physical and mental health are prioritized in development plans.

Focus Areas


  • Physical health
  • Mental health
  • Healthcare innovations
  • Community well-being

Quality Education


Stresses the necessity of providing equitable, high-quality education for all, fostering lifelong learning and preparing a skilled workforce for the future.

Focus Areas


  • Inclusive education
  • Remote learning
  • Lifelong learning
  • Teacher training

Economic Empowerment


Aims to uplift communities by promoting financial literacy, entrepreneurship, and vocational skills, driving inclusive economic growth.

Focus Areas


  • Financial literacy
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Vocational training
  • Economic development

Smart and Resilient Communities


Encourages the development of adaptive and innovative communities capable of withstanding and thriving amid future challenges and disasters.

Focus Areas


  • Smart cities
  • Disaster management
  • Infrastructure development
  • Social innovation

Cultural Heritage and Creativity


Recognizes the value of preserving cultural heritage while promoting creativity and the arts as essential components of a vibrant society.

Focus Areas


  • Preserving cultural heritage
  • Promoting arts and crafts
  • Fostering creativity.

Others


Encourages innovative solutions in any other area that aligns with the vision of Viksit Bharat 2047, fostering a culture of broad-based development and progress.

Focus Areas


  • Any other Area that does not come under the above mentioned theme but broadly qualifies for the vision of Viksit bharat.

Frequently asked questions

Who can participate in the SIM 2024?
Students from both ATL and Non-ATL schools are eligible to participate. Non-ATL schools can collaborate with ATL schools to form teams.
What are the rewards for the top teams?
The top teams will receive internships through the Student Innovator Program with leading corporates of India, certificates from AIM, NITI Aayog, and many more interesting opportunities at the conclusion of the Marathon.
What is the deadline for submitting entries?
The last date to submit entries is 26th January 2025, 11:59 PM.
Will all participating students receive a certificate?
Yes, all participating students will receive a certificate of Participation from AIM, NITI Aayog.
Can a team submit entries for more than one problem statement?
Yes, a team may submit entries for more than one problem statement. However, a separate application form must be filled out for each problem statement.
